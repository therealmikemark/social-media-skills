# The Desire Loop

The strategy framework that drives every thumbnail concept. Before generating
any concept, work the loop for the specific viewer of this specific video. The
loop turns a topic into a *click-worthy emotional promise*, not just a label.

## The four parts

1. **Desired Outcome** — what does the viewer ultimately want?
   The end state they are chasing. State it as a concrete, vivid result, not a
   category. ("A flat stomach in a month", not "fitness".)

2. **Pain Point** — what are they struggling with right now?
   The friction, frustration, or fear keeping them from the outcome. The sharper
   and more specific, the more the thumbnail can agitate it. ("Doing endless
   cardio and the belly fat won't budge.")

3. **Solution** — what promise does this video make?
   The bridge from pain to outcome. The mechanism, shortcut, or reframe the
   video delivers. This is what the thumbnail implicitly or explicitly offers.

4. **Curiosity Gap** — what unanswered question forces the click?
   The single most important lever. The thumbnail should open a loop the brain
   needs closed. Withhold the *how* or the *what*, show the stakes. A good gap
   is a question the viewer cannot answer by looking, only by clicking.

## How the loop maps onto the image

- The **Curiosity Gap** drives the *hook text* (3-5 words) and the focal element.
- The **Pain Point** drives the *emotional tone* (the expression on the face).
- The **Desired Outcome** drives the *payoff visual* (the after, the number, the
  transformation, the proof).
- The **Solution** is usually implied, not spelled out — spelling it out closes
  the gap and kills the click.

## Worked examples

**"How to Lose 10 Pounds of Visceral Fat in 30 Days"**
- Outcome: a visibly flatter stomach, fast.
- Pain: cardio and dieting that never touches the deep belly fat.
- Solution: a specific 30-day protocol.
- Gap: *what is the one thing that actually burns visceral fat?*
- → Hook text "It's Not Cardio", shocked/knowing expression, before/after silhouette.

**"This Marketer Replaced a 20-Person Team with Claude Code"**
- Outcome: same output, none of the headcount/cost.
- Pain: drowning in marketing tasks and payroll.
- Solution: an agent stack doing the work.
- Gap: *how can one person possibly replace twenty?*
- → Hook text "I Fired My Team" with a struck-through "20", confident expression,
  a dashboard or agent grid as the payoff visual.

## Output of the strategy step

For each of the **four concepts**, write one line per loop element, then derive:
- the **hook phrase** (3-5 words, from the gap),
- the **expression** (from the pain/outcome),
- the **focal payoff element** (from the outcome),
- a **one-line distinct angle** so the four concepts genuinely differ (e.g. number-led,
  before/after, contrarian "you're doing it wrong", authority/proof).
